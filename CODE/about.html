About Us

What We Do

We are a group of professional and amateur volunteer seamstresses who are committed to donating high-quality masks and personal protective equipment (PPE) to those on the frontline.

Our group is rapidly growing. We currently have over 80 Maskateers sewings, cutting, and delivering PPE to local Connecticut and Rhode Island front-line workers, hospitals, police, and military.

We have already donated thousands of masks to organizations such as Yale Hospital Systems, Smilow Cancer Hospital Care Centers, Visiting Nurse Association of South Eastern Connecticut, Hartford Health Systems, Middlesex Health Sytems, and many more!

Our Process

We have simplified the mask-making process in order to produce quality cotton masks quickly.

We make pre-cut kits with all the supplies needed to make 25 masks and distributing them to our sewing members. We then collect, quality control, and sterilize the masks before dropping them off to the organizations that are most in need.

Our team consists of several specialized groups:

Maskateers – Our fabulous sewers!

Zippers – A supply and quality control manager within each zip code that serves as the point of contact for that region.

Fast Pack – A kits with pre-cut materials needed to make 25 masks

Zip Lines – All the Maskateers within a zipcode

Marvelous Mavins – The fabric cutters that prepare pre-cut materials kits (Fast Packs)!

Strippers – Members who cut strips and make bias tape for the pre-cut kits (Fast Packs)!
